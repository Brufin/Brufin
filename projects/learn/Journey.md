# Journey

## Where does the data come from?

I had a goal in mind, to make a SOC triage lab, something that lead me to understand the pipeline of how SIEM operates. First question that came to my mind is *"How is this data collected?"* 

The "data" or telemetry comes from different endpoints and network devices. These devices create a substantial amount of data in the form of logs and events. During my research I found that there are different types of logs:

- *Security Logs:* Monitor access control and user behavior events.
- *System Logs:* Tracks operational information about the underlying system (e.g., hardware issues, service activities).
- *Application Logs:* Cover events generated by software programs and business applications.
- *Network Infrastructure Logs:* Monitors traffic and activity across network devices like routers and firewalls.

## The Big Picture

After learning about the different types of logs something clicked, it is all about visibility. We can't protect what we can't see, a threat actor might be living on your network completely undetected, if you aren't collecting telemetry.
